# Glossary

Stage management vocabulary used in Standby's docs, data model, and (eventually) the UI. Curated from the research pass; reads of textbooks (Kelly, Kincman) and college SM handbooks would expand this.

## Roles

- **SM (Stage Manager)** — owns paperwork, communication, calling the show. Reports to the director in rehearsal, owns the show in performance.
- **PSM (Production Stage Manager)** — senior SM on a multi-SM show; manages other SMs.
- **ASM (Assistant Stage Manager)** — supports the SM; often runs deck (backstage) during performance, owns prop preset/strike, costume tracking, run sheets.
- **DSM (Deputy Stage Manager)** — UK term; equivalent to the on-book / line-feeding ASM in the US.
- **Caller** — whoever calls cues; usually the SM, sometimes an ASM on large shows.

## Workflow phases

- **Pre-production** — script analysis, building paperwork, no actors yet.
- **Rehearsal** — actor-in-room phase; blocking, line work, design integration.
- **Tech week (tech)** — first time onstage with lights/sound/scenery. Includes:
  - **Q2Q (Cue-to-Cue)** — first tech rehearsal; skips between cues only.
  - **Dry tech** — tech without actors.
  - **Wet tech** — full tech with actors.
- **Previews** — public performances before opening, still adjusting.
- **Performance / run** — open through closing.
- **Strike** — taking down the production.

## Paperwork artifacts

- **Prompt book** — the SM's master binder. Holds the script (one-sided so facing page is blank for notes), blocking, cues, contacts, schedules, reports.
- **Calling script** — the prompt book in its performance state; marked with cues.
- **Rehearsal report** — nightly email to production team. Attendance, time breakdown, departmental notes.
- **Show report (performance report)** — same shape as rehearsal report but for performances. Includes house count, run times, holds, incidents.
- **Daily call / call sheet** — tomorrow's schedule sent the night before.
- **Line notes** — corrections to actors who paraphrased, dropped, or added lines.
- **Contact sheet** — phone + email for cast, creative, crew, admin.
- **Prop list** — master list of all props with sourcing status.
- **Costume plot** — matrix of who wears what in which scene.
- **French scene breakdown** — matrix of characters × scenes (split at every entrance/exit, not just at script's scene boundaries). The "source of truth" that should drive other paperwork.
- **Run sheet** — per-crew-position list of what to do during the show.
- **Preset list** — what's on stage before show starts.
- **Spike-mark plot** — tape marks on the deck for furniture positioning.

## Calling protocol

- **Warning** — optional. "Warning LX 47" — alerts the operator a cue is coming up soon.
- **Standby** — mandatory. "Standby LX 47 and Sound Q." Operator acknowledges ("LX standing by, Sound standing by").
- **GO** — only legal trigger word. "LX 47… GO." Always preceded by Standby. Never spoken in conversation in the booth.
- **Visual** — used in musicals when calling a music cue: "Visual to conductor" — the SM nods or signals.

## Blocking notation

- **X** — cross. "X DSR" = cross downstage right.
- **DSL, DSR, USL, USR, CS** — downstage left/right, upstage left/right, center stage. From the *actor's* perspective looking out to the audience.
- **ENT, EX** — enter, exit.
- **R, L** — right, left (actor's perspective).
- **OP, PS** — opposite prompt, prompt side (UK convention). PS = stage right (where the SM's prompt corner is, traditionally).
- **Boxed letter** — character symbol. ⬜H = Hamlet.
- **Numbered annotation** — "①" in the script with "① H X DSR to chair" on facing page.

## Cue types (department abbreviations)

- **LX** — lighting (from "electrics," the old term).
- **SD / SQ** — sound (sound cue).
- **FLY** — fly system (scenery flown in/out from above).
- **AUTO** — automation (motorized scenery, lifts, turntables).
- **DECK** — manual scene change cue.
- **FOH** — front of house (lobby, audience).
- **PYRO** — pyrotechnics (fire, sparks).
- **PROJ / VID** — projections / video.
- **STAR TRAP** — trapdoor stunt (rare; included for completeness).

## Equity terms

- **AEA (Actors' Equity Association)** — the US union for actors and stage managers.
- **Equity / non-Equity** — shorthand for "union" vs. "non-union" productions. SMs in college programs are non-Equity but learn Equity conventions.
- **URTA** — University/Resident Theatre Association; provides bridge from grad programs to professional credit.
- **5-and-5** — Equity rule: 5-min break per 55 minutes of work.
- **10-out-of-12** — Equity tech-rehearsal rule: 10 hours of rehearsal in a 12-hour day, allowed during tech week.

## Other terms

- **Virtual Callboard (VCB)** — proprietary web tool by EmptySpace Technology at [virtualcallboard.com](https://www.virtualcallboard.com/). Subscription-priced ($20-$150/mo). Distributes calls, reports, schedules; tracks attendance and read receipts. Used by Ball State and Lycoming, among others. Replaces the old physical bulletin call board outside the rehearsal room.
- **Call board** — historically, the physical bulletin board outside the rehearsal room where the SM posted the daily call, the sign-in sheet, and the current schedule. Largely replaced by email or by tools like VCB.
- **Half hour** — "the half" — 30 minutes before curtain. SM's first call to the cast. Then "fifteen," "five," "places."
- **Places** — "Places, please" — actors take their starting positions for top of show.
- **On book** — holding the script to feed lines. Pre-line-memorization actors are "off book" when they've memorized.
- **Holds** — pauses during a performance (technical issue, medical issue, audience disruption). Tracked in the show report.
- **Set** — confirms readiness. "House set?" "House is set."
- **House** — the audience, or the auditorium. "House open" = audience let in.
- **Dark night** — a scheduled night off during a run.
- **Cue light** — a backstage light system (red = standby, green = go) for cues that can't use headset.
- **Cans** — slang for the SM/crew comms headset.
- **God mic** — talkback mic from booth to stage during rehearsal.

## Documents formats / industry references

- **USITT** — United States Institute for Theatre Technology. Publishes sample paperwork formats considered standard.
- **SMA** — Stage Managers' Association. Professional service org for SMs.
- **Stern & Gold** — Lawrence Stern and Jill Gold, *Stage Management*, 12th edition (Routledge, 2022). The long-running practical/forms-driven textbook taught at Ball State and many BFA programs. Standby's form structures align to this book.
- **Porter & Alcorn** — Lisa Porter and Narda E. Alcorn, *Stage Management Theory as a Guide to Practice: Cultivating a Creative Approach*, 2nd edition (Focal Press / Routledge, 2023). The equity-and-inclusion-centered SM pedagogy text. Standby's default note phrasing aligns to this book's tone.
- **Off Headset** — Rafael Jaen and Christopher Sadler, eds., *Off Headset: Essays on Stage Management Work, Life, and Career* (Routledge, 2022). Anthology of essays by working SMs across regional, Broadway, opera, cruise, circus.
- **Kelly** — Thomas A. Kelly, *The Back Stage Guide to Stage Management*. Standard SM textbook (Penguin Random House). Not used at Ball State but widely cited.
- **Kincman** — Laurie Kincman, *The Stage Manager's Toolkit* (Routledge). Modern BFA-program textbook used at some schools.
